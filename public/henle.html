Redesigning the Henle Library for Android

G. Henle Verlag are sheet music publishers who specialise in publishing Urtext scores. These scores are considered to be the closest to the intended vision of the original composer and are the result of hours of research through the many different transciptions of the sheet music that have been written over the years.

With the introduction of the iPad, Henle wanted to start providing their scores to their customers digitally. Not content with just mirroring their physical counterparts, Henle wanted to take advantage of these new techonologies to improve interactivity, portability and flexibility for the musician.

The result was the Henle Library, both an advanced score reader and an online store for Henle’s customers to purchase Urtext scores. After the success of the iOS app Henle decided to develop an Android version to make the app avaible to a wider audience. I was tasked with re-designing the app to feel natural to an Android user.

One of the major aims was to try and align the app closer to Google’s material design guidelines so that Android users would have an experience closer to the the apps they normally use. The most obvious example of this was combining the navigation bar and bottom bar into one action bar as the guidelines at that point discouraged the use of bottom bars as they conflicted with the three navigation buttons on the device.

Another issue that we encounted is that the store and library on iOS used a tab switcher to switch between the two modes. Unfortunately Android’s stack based memory management system doesn’t easily allow for this kind of parallel page management.

To work around this we created a custom action bar featuring a tab switcher at the home level of the store and the library, then if the user navigated deeper the tab switcher was replaced with the traditional back button which let the user navigate back up the stack to the home level where they could switch.
